Business model

Three revenue streams, one compounding network

Three revenue streams, each reinforcing the next. Every airport we advise becomes a location we operate. Every location we operate feeds our platform.

Stream 1
Consulting
Strategic and technical advisory to airports, governments, and OEMs. From energy strategy and permitting to charging specification and project management. Every engagement builds a long-term airport relationship and a future network position.
Stream 2
Hardware margin
NRG2fly sources, supplies, and installs charging hardware. We earn a margin on procurement and installation. As the network grows across Europe, purchasing volumes increase and margins improve.
Stream 3
CPO and MSP
As Charge Point Operator (CPO), NRG2fly owns and manages the chargers and earns per kWh delivered. As Mobile Service Provider (MSP), NRG2fly operates the software platform through which pilots, operators, and ground handlers access and pay for the network.

What we charge

NRG2fly charges everything electric at and around airports, from the aircraft themselves to the vehicles that service them.

Electric aircraft — GA
General aviation, training flights, air taxis. Pipistrel Velis Electro, Bristell, H55 and other OEM partners. First charging sessions operational today.
Electric aircraft — commuter
Regional routes with 9–19 passengers. Vaeridion, Elysian, Beta and other scale-up OEMs. Market entry expected 2030–2033.
Ground service equipment
Electric baggage carts, pushback tractors, airside vehicles. Maastricht Airport (MAA) is an active trajectory. Large market, immediately profitable.
Landside charging
Electric cars for staff and visitors. Den Helder Airport operational today. Direct CPO revenue, strengthens the airport relationship.
We significantly reduce market timing risk. Whatever the pace of adoption — driven by certification, regulation, political will, OEM market entry, or airport transformation — NRG2fly builds compounding value at every stage. The network grows regardless of when the market peaks.

CPO & MSP margins improve at scale
Early network: ~30% margin per kWh delivered — fixed costs (hardware depreciation, grid connections, platform) spread across low volume. As the network scales and kWh volumes grow, those fixed costs are amortised across more sessions. By full scale, margins rise to ~50%+. The revenue ranges above are top-line; the margin uplift compounds the return.
