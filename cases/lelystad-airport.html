The Context

A Schiphol Group airport ready to lead in electric aviation

As a Schiphol Group airport, Lelystad has the institutional backing, the ambition and the runway capacity to become a serious base for electric flight. What was missing was the infrastructure to support it: reliable, public airside charging that works for both electric aircraft and ground support equipment.

NRG2fly was asked to design and build that infrastructure — a public airside charging plaza that serves both aircraft and GSE from a single location.

Our Role

Designed, built, tested — and handed over

NRG2fly was involved at every stage: from initial feasibility and grid connection advice, through charger selection and placement, to physical installation and commissioning. The charging plaza was designed to be future-proof — scalable capacity, a behind-the-meter setup where needed, and smart charging software via the TAP Electric App that lets pilots start and stop sessions directly from their phone.

We integrated TAP Electric as the session management layer and tested the full setup together with them until the system ran reliably. Once operational and proven, we handed the Charge Point Operator role to Lelystad Airport itself — and continue to assist them where needed.

The Result

Operational, public, and fully integrated

The charging plaza at Lelystad is live. Electric aircraft taxi in, plug in, charge — and take off again. The same infrastructure serves the ground equipment that keeps the airport running. One location, one system.

After extensive testing with TAP Electric, NRG2fly handed operational control to Lelystad Airport. The airport now runs the CPO role themselves — and we stay on hand to support.

Broader Impact

A blueprint for every airport that follows

Lelystad proves that public airside charging works — technically, operationally, commercially. The model NRG2fly developed here — combined aircraft and GSE charging, integrated CPO services, smart app-based session management — is now the template we bring to airports across Europe.

Every airport that wants to prepare for electric aviation has the same core questions: Where do we start? How do we finance it? Who operates it? Lelystad is the answer.

Ongoing Collaboration

Strategic consulting for the full electrification of Lelystad Airport

The handover of the charging plaza was not the end of our involvement — it was the start of something broader. NRG2fly is currently working with Lelystad Airport on a strategic consulting trajectory aimed at electrifying the airport as a whole: from apron operations and ground handling to long-term energy planning.

This is a close partnership, built on years of trust and shared ambition. We work directly with the airport team to think through what full electrification means in practice — technically, commercially, and operationally.

The project benefits from strong regional backing: Province Flevoland is actively involved as a regional partner, and Horizon Flevoland — one of NRG2fly's investors — is invested in seeing the region lead in sustainable aviation. Together, we're building something that goes well beyond a single charging plaza.
