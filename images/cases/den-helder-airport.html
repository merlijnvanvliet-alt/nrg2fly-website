Case Study Case Study — Den Helder Airport

Three days of craft

Airside infrastructure upgrade for electric aviation — zero disruption to airport operations

Sometimes the best preparation for the future is simply three days of solid work. At Den Helder Airport, we executed a complex infrastructure operation that brings the airport one step closer to electric aviation — without a single minute of disruption to operations.

The Challenge

Grid congestion is not a reason to wait

Den Helder Airport wants to be ready for electric aircraft and VTOLs. Like most regional airports, grid congestion until 2031 stands in the way. Waiting for the grid is not an option.

NRG2fly advised a behind-the-meter approach: install new airside cabling while the airport continues operating normally. That required a temporary power takeover of the main building — a technically complex operation where safety and continuity had to go hand in hand.

Our Role

Strategic sparring partner & independent technical advisor

NRG2fly acts as strategic sparring partner and independent technical advisor. With an external, unbiased perspective we help airports make decisions that hold up long-term — technically, operationally and financially.

Ed Meijer leads this type of project. Years of experience in airport infrastructure and electrical systems make him the right person to direct a consortium of specialised parties — while standing alongside the internal technical team as a trusted advisor.

The Execution

Step by step — zero downtime

We temporarily took over the power supply of the main building using two parallel-connected Bredenoord generators of 200 kVA each — fully redundant, zero interruption.

  1. 1 Solar inverters taken offlinePV inverters negatively affect the voltage and frequency regulation of generators. Critical first step.
  2. 2 SNO performedGenerators synchronised and connected in parallel to the grid.
  3. 3 HKL main switch isolatedSecured against re-energisation via LOTO procedure.
  4. 4 Liander transformer knife fuses releasedComplete separation from the public grid.
  5. 5 New airside cabling connectedKenter, Liander, Taylor Groep, and Lindeman as switchboard builder.
  6. 6 Meijer GrondbedrijfTrench work and full pavement restoration after cabling completion.
  7. 7 Den Helder AirportKey management and on-site supervision throughout the operation.
  8. 8 Switching back to gridAfter testing and completion, via controlled SNO procedure.

The Result

Foundation laid — connected as CPO

The infrastructure for charging electric aircraft and VTOLs at Den Helder Airport is taking concrete shape. Despite grid congestion until 2031, a foundation has been laid that matters.

NRG2fly is connected to Den Helder Airport as Charge Point Operator (CPO) — involved from strategy to SLA.
